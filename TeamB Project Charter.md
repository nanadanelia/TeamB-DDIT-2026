# Georgian Grocery Retail Digital Ecosystem Transformation (Phase 1)

## Core Problem

The Georgian grocery market is gradually shifting toward **digital and platform-based consumption**, where delivery platforms (Glovo, Wolt, Bolt Food) act as key access points to customers.

Nikora is already present on Glovo, but:
- lacks its own transactional digital platform  
- relies on third-party channels for digital demand  
- has limited control over customer experience and data  

As a result, Nikora risks losing **direct access to customers (“customer interface”)** to platform-based ecosystems.

---

## Governing Thought

The competitive model is evolving from **"Physical Store Density"** to **"Digital Accessibility & Platform Integration"**.

Winning in this environment requires:
- being easily accessible through digital channels  
- integrating ordering, delivery, and customer interaction  
- increasing control over customer relationships and data  

---

## Strategic Objectives

### 1. Strengthen Digital Accessibility

Ensure Nikora is fully accessible across key customer channels:
- Improve presence and performance on delivery platforms (Glovo, Wolt)
- Increase visibility and ease of ordering for digital customers

---

### 2. Build Transactional Digital Capability

Move beyond informational presence toward ownership of the customer journey:
- Develop own online ordering capability (app or website)
- Enable direct interaction with customers without full reliance on third-party platforms

---

### 3. Improve Operational Efficiency

Leverage digital channels to improve internal performance:
- Use demand data to improve inventory planning  
- Reduce inefficiencies in logistics and stock management  

---

### 4. Capture Convenience-Driven Demand

Position Nikora to compete for convenience-based consumption:
- Target urban customers using delivery platforms  
- Increase share of planned and convenience purchases  

---

## Success Metric

Success is defined as:

- Increased share of orders coming through digital channels  
- Improved accessibility and visibility on delivery platforms  
- Measurable growth in convenience-driven demand  

Within an initial **12–18 month implementation horizon**, Nikora should demonstrate clear progress in digital competitiveness.

---

## Key Strategic Insight

The challenge is not whether Nikora is present digitally, but whether its **current level of digital capability is sufficient to compete**.

👉 **Presence alone does not create advantage — integration and accessibility do.**
