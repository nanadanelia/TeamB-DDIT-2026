# Executive Summary — PSP Beauty & Wellness Ecosystem Platform

**Team:** Nana Danelia, Daviti Mtchedlishvili, Davit Kulua, Mariam Berekashvili
**Industry context:** Digital Healthcare, Pharmacy Retail, AI-Powered Wellness Ecosystem
**Date:** June 2026

---

## Three conclusions the board must act on

**Conclusion 1: Traditional pharmacy retail is losing differentiation and PSP must move now.**

Georgian pharmacy competition has converged. PSP, GPC, Aversi, and Pharmadepot offer comparable products, digital ordering, delivery, and loyalty schemes. At the same time, Wolt and Glovo already intermediate pharmacy delivery, and digital wellness platforms are capturing the skincare discovery journey before customers reach the pharmacy. PSP's competitive advantage is eroding in real time. The window for first-mover advantage in Georgian wellness AI personalization is approximately 18–24 months. After that, better-funded competitors or international platforms will enter with established AI capabilities and existing customer relationships. This means the board must approve and fund the ecosystem strategy now, not after a further feasibility review.

**Conclusion 2: The financial case is robust across all three scenarios — including the downside.**

Total 5-year cost of ownership is GEL 3.37M, comprising GEL 506K one-time implementation and GEL 592,9K annual operating costs. Against this, the ecosystem generates GEL 35.25M in incremental revenue over 5 years in the base case, built bottom-up from PSP's verified 1.9 million loyalty cardholders. The base-case payback period is approximately 16 months. The 5-year NPV at a 10% discount rate is GEL 21.8M. Even in the downside scenario — where ecosystem adoption reaches only 30% of projections — the 5-year NPV remains positive at GEL 5.8M and payback is achieved by Year 3. The investment is financially viable in all three scenarios. The risk is timing, not loss.

**Conclusion 3: Data governance is the single most critical dependency and must begin in Month 1.**

No AI recommendation engine, customer segmentation model, or personalized loyalty campaign can be deployed without clean customer data, a consent framework, and defined data ownership. If data governance is delayed, every H2 initiative is delayed with it. The board must approve data governance as the first funded initiative — before the MVP platform, before partnership activation, and before any AI commitment is made.

---

## Recommendation in one sentence

PSP should launch a phased AI-enabled personalized wellness ecosystem built on its existing loyalty infrastructure, dermatologist and influencer partnerships, and pharmacy credibility, beginning with a GEL 506K MVP deployment in Months 1–6 and expanding into AI-driven personalization in Months 7–18.

## ROI headline

| Metric | Downside | Base case | Upside |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total 5-year investment | GEL 3.37M | GEL 3.37M | GEL 3.37M |
| Total 5-year incremental revenue | GEL 10.6M | GEL 35.25M | GEL 52.9M |
| Payback period | ~Year 3 | ~16 months | ~Year 1 |
| 5-year NPV (10% discount rate) | GEL 5.8M | GEL 21.8M | GEL 33.5M |
| IRR | ~85% | ~320% | ~450%+ |

**Key assumption driving scenario difference:** Ecosystem adoption rate. Downside = 30% of base projections. Base case = 100%. Upside = 150%.

**What GEL 3.37M represents in context:** PSP's verified 2021 revenue is GEL 745M. The total 5-year ecosystem investment equals 0.45% of one year's revenue. PSP's annual advertising spend alone exceeds GEL 7.5M — more than the entire 5-year ecosystem cost of ownership.

**Revenue model basis:** 1.9M loyalty cardholders × 15% active app users = 285,000 active users × 60% wellness engagement = 171,000 wellness buyers × GEL 25 average monthly skincare spend × 12 months = GEL 51.3M annual skincare revenue base. AI cross-sell uplift of 10–20% on this base generates GEL 2.7M incremental revenue in Year 2, growing to GEL 6.5M by Year 5. Retention improvement, sponsored campaigns, advisor appointments, and premium subscriptions contribute the remaining GEL 6.6M in Year 5.

## Three decisions the board must make today

1. **Approve the phased ecosystem strategy and GEL 506K initial investment** — to begin MVP development, data governance, and partnership activation in Month 1.

2. **Appoint a Chief Digital Officer or Head of Ecosystem** with cross-functional authority over Digital, CRM, Marketing, Partnerships, and AI deployment — before Month 2.

3. **Prioritize data governance as the first funded workstream** — no AI deployment initiative proceeds until the unified customer profile, consent framework, and data ownership policy are approved and operational.

**Cost of a six-month delay:** MVP launch moves to Month 10. H2 AI deployment moves to Month 24. First-mover advantage in customer data accumulation and dermatologist partnership exclusivity is lost. Payback period extends by approximately 6 months in the base case.
