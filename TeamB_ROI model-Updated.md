## Part 1. Strategic Rationale

How should **Nikora** respond to **the growing shift of consumer demand toward delivery platforms and digital channels** in order to **remain competitive and capture incremental demand currently served by digitally-enabled competitors** before **competitors and delivery platforms capture a structurally larger share of the grocery market**?

- **Strategic rationale (two sentences):**  
Nikora currently lacks transactional digital capabilities — its mobile application is primarily informational and does not support online ordering, and the company has no visible presence on major delivery platforms such as Wolt and Glovo. At the same time, competitors (Carrefour, Spar) and delivery ecosystems are capturing increasing demand for convenience-driven grocery consumption, creating a structural competitive disadvantage for Nikora.

- **Connection to disruption diagnostic:**  
Consumer readiness for digital consumption is supported (H1), while competitors with delivery capabilities are gaining advantage over Nikora (H2).

- **Connection to competitive landscape:**  
Retail competition in Georgia is shifting from store density to accessibility across physical and digital channels; Nikora’s limited digital presence reduces its ability to compete for convenience-driven demand.

## Part 2. Value Drivers

### 2.1 Revenue Growth

- **Mechanism:**  
Because Nikora introduces online ordering channels (via partnerships and/or its own platform), it can capture demand currently served by delivery platforms and competitors, generating incremental revenue beyond physical store sales.

- **Scope of impact:**  
Urban consumers, convenience-driven purchases, and planned grocery shopping.

- **Order of magnitude estimate:**  
+2% to +5% incremental revenue within 3 years.

- **Data needed to quantify:**  
Nikora revenue baseline; online adoption rate; basket size comparison.

- **Evidence collected so far:**  
Delivery platforms (Wolt, Glovo) are widely used; competitors already offer digital access; grocery remains predominantly offline, indicating untapped demand.

---

### 2.2 Cost Reduction

- **Mechanism:**  
Digital channels generate demand data that improves inventory planning and reduces waste, while targeted promotions improve marketing efficiency.

- **Scope of impact:**  
Inventory, logistics, marketing.

- **Order of magnitude estimate:**  
1% – 3% efficiency improvement.

- **Evidence collected so far:**  
Industry benchmarks show digital grocery enables better forecasting and lower waste.

---

### 2.3 Risk Reduction

- **Risk being mitigated:**  
Loss of customer interface and demand to delivery platforms and digital competitors.

- **Mechanism:**  
By developing digital channels, Nikora maintains direct access to customers rather than relying solely on physical stores.

- **Scope of impact:**  
Customer retention, long-term competitiveness.

- **Order of magnitude estimate:**  
Potential margin erosion of 15–30% if fully dependent on third-party platforms.

- **Evidence collected so far:**  
Competitors already use delivery platforms; Nikora lacks presence; delivery ecosystems are expanding.

---

### 2.4 Strategic Optionality

- **Future opportunity enabled:**  
Personalized promotions, loyalty programs, data-driven pricing, and digital expansion.

- **Why not accessible today:**  
Nikora lacks transactional digital platform and customer-level data.

- **Mechanism:**  
Digital channels create a data feedback loop enabling better targeting and retention.

## Part 4. Key Assumptions

### Assumption 1

- **Statement:**  
Consumers will adopt online grocery channels at a level sufficient to generate +2–5% incremental revenue.

- **Basis:**  
Strong delivery platform usage and digital payment growth (H1 supported).

---

### Assumption 2

- **Statement:**  
Digital channels will generate incremental demand rather than fully cannibalizing offline sales.

- **Basis:**  
Different use cases for online vs in-store shopping (planned vs urgent purchases).

---

### Assumption 3

- **Statement:**  
Nikora can build or access digital capabilities at the estimated cost range.

- **Basis:**  
Benchmark estimates from similar projects.

---

### Assumption 4

- **Statement:**  
Delivery platform partnerships (Wolt, Glovo) remain available during transition.

## Part 5. Scenario Sketch

### Base Case

- **Description:**  
Nikora adopts a hybrid model (partnership + own platform), capturing moderate adoption and incremental revenue.

- **Payback:**  
4–5 years

---

### Upside Case

- **Description:**  
Strong adoption and successful scaling of digital channels.

- **Payback:**  
2.5–3.5 years

---

### Downside Case

- **Description:**  
Slow adoption, higher costs, and stronger competition from digital players.

- **Payback:**  
6–8 years

---

### Key Differentiating Assumption

- **Most critical factor:**  
Customer adoption of online grocery

- **Why uncertain:**  
No strong Georgia-specific precedent
