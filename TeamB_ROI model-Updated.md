## Part 1. Strategic Rationale

How should **Nikora** respond to **the accelerating shift of consumer spending toward digital platforms and cross-border e-commerce** in order to **build an integrated omnichannel ecosystem combining physical retail, digital platforms, logistics, and customer data** before **digital-native competitors and delivery aggregators capture enough of the consumer wallet to structurally displace traditional grocery retailers**?

- **Strategic rationale (two sentences):** Nikora operates in a market where e-commerce is growing at 40%+ annually and cross-border platforms such as Temu are capturing GEL 1.4 billion in Georgian consumer spending, threatening the relevance of store-density-based competition. If Nikora does not build digital capabilities now, it risks ceding customer interface, data, and logistics control to delivery aggregators and cross-border platforms that are compounding their advantages each year.
- **Connection to disruption diagnostic:** Cross-border e-commerce is the most strongly validated competitive threat (H3 ✅); digital payment adoption and delivery platform usage confirm consumer readiness for digital grocery (H1 ✅).
- **Connection to competitive landscape:** Leading chains Magniti and Daily compete via dense store networks; Nikora's weaker physical density makes digital an alternative competitive vector (H5 ⚠️), but this requires pilot validation.

## Part 2. Value Drivers

### 2.1 Revenue Growth

How does the transformation enable new revenue that would not otherwise exist?

- **Mechanism:** Because Nikora launches digital ordering channels (own platform and/or delivery partnerships), consumers who currently purchase online via cross-border platforms or skip grocery delivery entirely gain a local digital alternative, which produces incremental basket spend that would not have been captured through physical stores alone.
- **Scope of impact:** Convenience-driven and planned purchase segments; non-perishable and high-frequency grocery categories; Georgian consumers currently using Wolt/Glovo or cross-border platforms.
- **Order of magnitude estimate:** +2% to +5% additional sales within 3 years.
- **Data needed to quantify:** Nikora's current annual revenue baseline; average basket size for online vs. offline grocery; customer adoption rate assumptions by segment.
- **Evidence collected so far:** Grocery is ~92% offline, indicating large untapped digital demand (H2 ✅); delivery platform usage (Wolt, Glovo) confirms digital convenience demand exists; e-commerce growing at 40%+ annually.

---

### 2.2 Cost Reduction

How does the transformation reduce operating cost through automation, process improvement, or asset reduction?

- **Mechanism:** Because digital channels provide real-time demand data and customer purchase patterns, Nikora can optimize inventory replenishment and reduce overstock, which produces lower waste costs and improved margin per SKU.
- **Scope of impact:** Inventory management, logistics, and marketing targeting functions.
- **Order of magnitude estimate:** 1% – 3% efficiency improvement across relevant cost lines.
- **Data needed to quantify:** Current waste rate as % of COGS; current inventory carrying costs; baseline marketing spend and conversion rates.
- **Evidence collected so far:** Industry benchmarks indicate digital-enabled grocery retailers achieve meaningful waste reduction through demand forecasting; improved targeting reduces blended customer acquisition cost.

---

### 2.3 Risk Reduction

How does the transformation reduce the probability or impact of a material risk?

- **Risk being mitigated:** Structural displacement by cross-border e-commerce platforms (Temu, AliExpress) and delivery aggregators (Wolt, Glovo) capturing customer interface, data, and loyalty away from Nikora permanently.
- **Mechanism:** Because Nikora builds its own digital customer interface and data infrastructure, it retains direct relationships with consumers rather than becoming a fulfilment node for third-party platforms that own the customer.
- **Scope of impact:** Customer retention across all segments; long-term margin protection against aggregator fee pressure.
- **Order of magnitude estimate:** Risk of margin erosion from aggregator dependency estimated at 15–30% of online revenue if fully platform-dependent (industry benchmark).
- **Data needed to quantify:** Aggregator commission rates (Wolt/Glovo); share of Nikora revenue currently intermediated by third parties; customer churn rate in platform-dependent models.
- **Evidence collected so far:** Cross-border e-commerce reached GEL 1.4bn and is growing faster than domestic retail (H3 ✅); ecosystem power is shifting toward actors controlling customer interface, data, and logistics.

---

### 2.4 Strategic Optionality

How does the transformation create the capability to pursue future opportunities that are currently not accessible?

- **Future opportunity enabled:** Personalised loyalty programs, predictive replenishment, private label digital promotion, and potential expansion of the digital model to other Georgian grocery chains.
- **Why this capability is not accessible today:** Without a proprietary digital platform and customer data asset, Nikora cannot personalise offers, predict demand at the customer level, or monetise its customer relationships beyond the point-of-sale transaction.
- **Mechanism:** Building owned digital infrastructure creates a data flywheel: more digital transactions generate more behavioural data, which enables better targeting, which drives higher conversion and retention, compounding over time.
- **Order of magnitude estimate:** Option value not yet quantified; scalability to other Georgian chains represents a multiplier on the initial investment thesis.
- **Evidence collected so far:** Model is explicitly designed to be scalable beyond Nikora to other Georgian grocery chains; Jobs-to-be-done analysis confirms multi-channel consumer behaviour that rewards personalisation.

## Part 4. Key Assumptions

### Assumption 1 (most important)

- **Statement:** We assume that Georgian grocery consumers will adopt online ordering at a rate sufficient to generate +2% incremental revenue within 3 years, because delivery platform usage (Wolt, Glovo) and 40%+ e-commerce growth confirm latent digital demand for convenience.
- **Basis:** H1 partially validated; H2 likely valid; consumer readiness evidence from National Bank of Georgia payment statistics and delivery platform penetration data.
- **Impact if wrong by 20%:** Revenue uplift drops from +2–5% to +1.6–4%, extending the base case payback period from 4–5 years toward the downside scenario of 6–8 years.

---

### Assumption 2

- **Statement:** We assume that Nikora can capture incremental demand without full cannibalization of offline sales, because online grocery primarily serves planned and convenience purchase occasions that are currently unserved rather than substituting existing in-store trips.
- **Basis:** H2 validated — Jobs-to-be-done analysis shows consumers use different channels for different needs; offline dominates fresh and urgent purchases while online captures planned and non-perishable categories.
- **Impact if wrong by 20%:** Incremental revenue estimate reduces significantly if online orders primarily substitute offline baskets, compressing net revenue impact and degrading ROI.

---

### Assumption 3

- **Statement:** We assume that technology and implementation costs remain within GEL 3.6M – 8.4M, because this range is based on the initial ROI model estimate calibrated to Nikora's scale and a hybrid build-plus-partner approach.
- **Basis:** Initial ROI model estimate; to be stress-tested against regional grocery digitalization benchmarks in Weekend 2.
- **Impact if wrong by 20%:** Total investment rises to GEL 4.3M – 10.1M, extending base case payback by approximately 0.5–1 year and pushing downside scenario beyond 8 years.

---

### Additional assumptions

- **Assumption 4:** Delivery infrastructure partnerships (Wolt/Glovo) remain available on commercially viable terms during the transition period before Nikora's own logistics capability matures.
- **Assumption 5:** Cross-border platform growth (Temu, AliExpress) does not accelerate to the point of structurally collapsing domestic grocery demand before Nikora's digital capabilities are established.

## Part 5. Scenario Sketch

### 5.1 Base Case

- **Description:** Nikora launches a hybrid model (own platform + delivery partnerships), achieves moderate consumer adoption, and realises +2–3% incremental revenue and 1–2% cost efficiency improvement within 3 years.
- **Key assumption value used:** Customer adoption at industry median; technology costs at midpoint of range (GEL ~6M).
- **Rough payback period estimate:** 4 – 5 years.
- **Why this is the most defensible scenario:** Hybrid model (Option 3) balances speed and control; moderate adoption is supported by H1 partial validation; incremental demand thesis supported by H2.

---

### 5.2 Upside Case

- **Description:** Nikora's digital platform achieves rapid consumer adoption, cross-sell and loyalty effects accelerate, and the model is successfully scaled to additional Georgian grocery chains, generating +5% revenue uplift and 3% cost efficiency.
- **Key assumption value used:** Customer adoption at top quartile; successful model replication to 1–2 additional chains within 3 years.
- **Rough payback period estimate:** 2.5 – 3.5 years.
- **What has to go right:** High consumer adoption rate; successful own-platform development; logistics partnerships maintained; scalability thesis validated by pilot.

---

### 5.3 Downside Case

- **Description:** Consumer adoption is slower than expected, implementation costs overrun, and cross-border platform growth further compresses domestic grocery margins before Nikora's digital capabilities generate returns.
- **Key assumption value used:** Adoption at bottom quartile; implementation delay of 6+ months; costs at high end of range (GEL 8.4M+).
- **Rough payback period estimate:** 6 – 8 years.
- **Does the client survive this scenario?** Yes — offline grocery (~92% of revenue) continues to generate operating cash flow; digital investment is a capability build, not a survival dependency in the short term. However, long-term relevance risk increases materially if adaptation is delayed further.

---

### 5.4 Key Differentiating Assumption

- **The single assumption that most changes the outcome across scenarios:** Customer adoption rate of online grocery.
- **Why this assumption is the most uncertain:** Georgian online grocery is nascent; no direct local precedent for Nikora-scale adoption curves; consumer readiness is validated at the category level but individual retailer adoption rates are unproven (H5  unproven).
- **What research would reduce this uncertainty:** Pilot launch data; consumer survey on willingness to order grocery online from Nikora specifically; analysis of Wolt/Glovo basket data for grocery categories.
