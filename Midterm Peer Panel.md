# Activity: Midterm Peer Panel

## Purpose

Challenge the presenting team on the weakest elements of their midterm submission. A strong challenge earns participation points. A clarifying question does not count as a challenge.

## Duration
10 minutes Q&A per presentation

## Session
Saturday Block 3, 13:30 to 15:00

---

## Panel assignments

| Presenting team | Panel challenger 1 | Panel challenger 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Team A | Team B challenges ROI model | Team C challenges strategic options |
| Team B | Team C challenges ROI model | Team A challenges industry analysis |
| Team C | Team A challenges ROI model | Team B challenges recommendation logic |

---

## Four challenge types

Use one of these four forms. Do not ask clarifying questions during Q&A.

### 1. Number challenge
Format: "Your base case payback is [X] months. Your adoption rate assumption is [A]. What happens to the payback period if adoption is 30 percent lower than your base case?"

Apply this when: the ROI model has a single scenario or the key assumption has not been stress-tested.

### 2. Structural distinction challenge
Format: "Option 2 adds a digital channel to your existing business. Option 1 defends the existing business. What is structurally different about the value proposition of Option 2? Not the investment size. The value proposition."

Apply this when: two of the three options appear to be budget levels of the same strategy rather than structurally distinct options.

### 3. Recommendation challenge
Format: "You recommend Option 2. What is the single external condition that would cause you to recommend Option 1 instead? Be specific. Not 'general market uncertainty.'"

Apply this when: the recommendation is stated without a condition of change.

### 4. Implementation challenge
Format: "Your roadmap shows data governance in month 3 and AI deployment in month 4. What specifically is completed in month 3 that makes month 4 viable?"

Apply this when: the roadmap has adjacent items with no explicit dependency stated.

---

## Peer scoring

Each peer team submits a score for the criterion they are assigned.

| Criterion | Points | Assigned to |
|---|---|---|
| Industry analysis | 7 | Assigned per panel |
| Strategic options | 7 | Assigned per panel |
| Financial rigor | 6 | Assigned per panel |
| Implementation logic | 5 | Assigned per panel |

Submit your score to the instructor on the scoring sheet before the next team presents.

---

## Rules
- One challenge per peer team per Q&A session.
- Challenges must be specific. Name a number, a slide, or a stated claim.
- The presenting team must answer without consulting the deck.
- The instructor may redirect or follow up after peer challenges.
