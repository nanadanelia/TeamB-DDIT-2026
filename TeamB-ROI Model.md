# ROI Model — PSP Beauty & Wellness Ecosystem Platform

## Purpose

Evaluate the strategic and financial feasibility of building a PSP-centered digital beauty, wellness, and preventive health ecosystem platform in Georgia.

The model is designed as a **strategic transformation investment model**, not as a precise corporate valuation. Financial estimates are therefore based on:

- publicly available market data
- benchmark implementations
- comparable omnichannel retail and wellness platforms
- Georgian digital market context
- existing PSP infrastructure and capabilities

All figures in USD millions.

---

# Strategic Context

PSP already possesses several structural advantages that reduce implementation complexity relative to a greenfield startup:

- 350–400+ branches across Georgia
- large existing loyalty and customer base
- existing e-commerce and delivery operations
- Microsoft Dynamics 365 + LS Retail infrastructure already implemented
- strong brand recognition and trust within Georgia

These existing assets reduce implementation risk and allow partial reuse of operational and technological infrastructure.

# Part 1. Investment Structure (Year 0)

## Investment Philosophy

The proposed platform should not be evaluated purely as a short-term transactional e-commerce project.

A significant portion of the investment creates long-term strategic digital capabilities including:

- ecosystem infrastructure
- personalization systems
- creator and expert integration
- customer engagement systems
- recommendation capabilities
- customer data ownership
- ecosystem positioning

The investment logic therefore follows a **Three Horizons transformation model**, where different investments generate value at different stages of ecosystem maturity.

## Investment Logic & Benchmark Basis

The investment estimate is considered realistic because PSP already possesses:

- existing ERP infrastructure
- operational retail systems
- digital commerce capabilities
- loyalty systems
- fulfillment infrastructure
- nationwide pharmacy network

This reduces implementation complexity compared to a fully greenfield digital platform.

Comparable omnichannel retail and wellness implementations in Eastern Europe using Microsoft Dynamics and LS Retail infrastructure demonstrate that ecosystem-oriented retail transformation increasingly relies on integrated ERP, CRM, loyalty, and e-commerce systems rather than standalone transactional applications.

# Part 3. Scenario Framework

## Key Differentiating Variable

The main scenario differentiator is:

### Platform Engagement Rate

Defined as:

> Percentage of PSP customers who become recurring ecosystem users engaging with personalized recommendations, wellness content, creator interaction, educational features, or digital purchasing behavior.

---

| Scenario | Platform Engagement Rate by Year 3 | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Downside | 3–5% | Slow ecosystem adoption |
| Base Case | 7–10% | Moderate but realistic ecosystem adoption |
| Upside | 12–15% | Strong ecosystem engagement and creator traction |

---

## Why These Assumptions Are Considered Reasonable

The assumptions are based on:

- high social media usage among Georgian urban consumers
- increasing digital wellness and skincare engagement
- strong PSP brand recognition
- existing customer trust
- already established influencer-pharmacy collaborations
- rising online beauty and wellness purchasing behavior
- existing PSP digital infrastructure

At the same time, assumptions remain conservative because:

- the Georgian market remains relatively small
- online wellness ecosystems are still emerging
- consumer behavior transition requires time
- ecosystem engagement develops gradually rather than immediately

# Part 4. Horizon-Based ROI Logic

## Horizon 1 — Ecosystem Foundation (0–18 Months)

### Strategic Objective

Build foundational ecosystem capabilities and establish PSP’s digital beauty and wellness positioning.

### Main Value Drivers

- platform adoption
- ecosystem participation
- creator partnerships
- digital engagement
- customer data collection
- loyalty integration
- visibility within wellness journeys

### ROI Interpretation

At Horizon 1, ROI should primarily be evaluated through:

- engagement growth
- ecosystem adoption
- repeat platform interaction
- creator participation
- customer acquisition efficiency
- digital ecosystem positioning

rather than direct short-term financial return.

---

## Horizon 2 — Ecosystem Expansion & Monetization (18–36 Months)

### Strategic Objective

Expand from transactional retail toward ecosystem-driven beauty and wellness engagement.

### Main Value Drivers

- increased repeat purchasing
- higher customer lifetime value
- stronger retention
- personalized recommendation systems
- cross-category wellness purchasing
- ecosystem-driven customer behavior

### ROI Interpretation

At Horizon 2, ecosystem investments begin generating measurable commercial value through:

- increased purchase frequency
- higher basket size
- retention improvement
- stronger digital participation
- ecosystem-driven purchasing behavior

---

## Horizon 3 — Ecosystem Leadership & Long-Term Monetization (36+ Months)

### Strategic Objective

Position PSP as a leading digital beauty, wellness, and preventive health ecosystem in Georgia.

### Main Value Drivers

- ecosystem lock-in
- advanced personalization
- long-term customer relationships
- scalable ecosystem monetization
- platform-scale loyalty
- partnership and subscription opportunities

### ROI Interpretation

Horizon 3 represents the stage where ecosystem investments generate stronger long-term financial returns through:

- customer lifetime value expansion
- retention economics
- ecosystem monetization
- platform-based competitive advantage
- sustainable differentiation

---

# Strategic Financial Interpretation

The project should not be interpreted as a traditional short-term retail ROI project.

A significant portion of the investment creates strategic digital capabilities whose value compounds across later horizons rather than generating immediate direct financial return.

Therefore:

👉 Horizon 1 ROI is primarily ecosystem-building ROI.  
👉 Horizon 2 ROI is ecosystem monetization ROI.  
👉 Horizon 3 ROI is long-term platform and competitive advantage ROI.
