# Issue Tree v2 — Nikora Digital Strategy

## Core Question

What is the optimal digital strategy for Nikora to maintain market share and profitability in the Georgian grocery market under increasing online and delivery competition?

---

## Why this question matters

The Georgian grocery retail market is undergoing disruption due to:
- Increasing adoption of delivery platforms (Wolt, Glovo, Bolt Food)
- Rising consumer expectations for speed and convenience
- Growing price transparency and competition (including cross-border platforms)
- Expansion of digital capabilities among competitors (Carrefour, Spar)
- Pressure on margins due to logistics and supply chain complexity

---

## Part 3. Hypothesis Extraction

### Hypothesis 1 (Demand)

Statement:  
Urban Georgian consumers show strong adoption of delivery services, indicating growing readiness for online grocery demand.

Why plausible:  
Rapid growth of Wolt, Glovo, and Bolt Food suggests increasing demand for convenience.

---

### Hypothesis 2 (Competition)

Statement:  
Competitors with integrated digital and delivery capabilities are gaining market share compared to store-only retailers.

Why plausible:  
Carrefour and Spar already offer digital ordering, while delivery platforms expand rapidly.

---

### Hypothesis 3 (Economics)

Statement:  
Online grocery increases average basket size but introduces significant last-mile delivery costs that pressure margins.

Why plausible:  
Global benchmarks show higher basket sizes but high logistics costs.

---

### Hypothesis 4 (Capabilities)

Statement:  
Nikora’s current capabilities (store network, logistics, digital infrastructure) are insufficient to compete effectively in online grocery without strategic changes.

Why plausible:  
Limited visible digital presence compared to competitors.
