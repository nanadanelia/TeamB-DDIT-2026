# Disruption Diagnostic v1: Georgian Retail vs. Cross-Border E-Commerce

Using Christensen’s Logic to evaluate the threat of Cross-Border E-Commerce (Temu/Trendyol) vs. Georgian Retail.

---

## Part 1. Define the Incumbent Market

**Industry:** Georgian Organized Grocery/FMCG Retail  

**Main incumbent firms:** Nikora, Ori Nabiji, Carrefour, SPAR  

**Traditional business model:** High-density physical store networks with central distribution  

**Primary basis of competition:** Physical proximity ("Neighborhood store") and fresh assortment  

---

## Part 2. Define the Challenger / Technology Shift

**Challenger technology:** Cross-border Algorithmic E-commerce (Temu/Trendyol)  

**Initial offering:** Ultra-low-cost non-perishable household goods and apparel  

**Initial target users:** Price-sensitive urban consumers (18-45) willing to trade 2-week delivery for 50%+ savings  

**Why it seemed threatening:** +56.7% YoY growth and a GEL 1.4bn market capture in 2024-2025  

---

## Part 3. Entry Pattern

- **Low-end market:** Yes — they serve "overserved" customers who don't need premium store ambiance or immediate service for household staples  
- **New-market foothold:**  No — most users were already consumers, switching budgets rather than entering the market for the first time  
- **Rational to ignore initially:** Yes — incumbents ignored them because "Grocery is about freshness and 15-minute delivery," which cross-border cannot deliver  

---

## Part 4. Performance Trajectory

- **Improvement:** Logistics speed improving from 21 days → 7-10 days via local hubs  
- **Mainstream move:** Expanding from gadgets to high-frequency FMCG items (cleaning supplies, diapers)  
- **Change:** Drastic reduction in cost-to-consumer by bypassing local distributor margins  

---

## Part 5. Classification

**Classification:** Low-end disruption moving toward Architectural Shift  

**Justification:**  
While it started as low-end disruption (cheaper goods), the integration with digital payments and global supply chains is an Architectural Shift. It fundamentally changes how the "Grocery Job" is unbundled: fresh food stays local (Sustaining), but staples go global (Disruptive).
