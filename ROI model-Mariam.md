# Part 1. Strategic Rationale

How should PSP respond to the growing shift of consumer attention, product discovery, and wellness purchasing toward digital platforms, influencer ecosystems, and personalized online experiences in order to strengthen long-term customer engagement and capture higher-value beauty and wellness demand before specialized digital platforms and ecosystem players capture structurally larger portions of the customer relationship?

- **Strategic rationale (two sentences):**  
PSP already possesses strong brand trust, pharmacy infrastructure, customer reach, and loyalty capabilities, but most beauty and wellness discovery increasingly occurs outside PSP’s ecosystem through Instagram, TikTok, influencers, and specialized e-commerce platforms. As digital-first shopping behavior grows, PSP risks remaining primarily a transactional fulfillment layer while customer attention, engagement, personalization, and recommendation authority increasingly shift toward external ecosystem players.

- **Connection to disruption diagnostic:**  
Consumer behavior is increasingly shifting toward personalized, creator-driven, and digitally integrated beauty and wellness experiences, while specialized online ecosystems and influencers increasingly shape purchasing decisions.

- **Connection to competitive landscape:**  
Competition in beauty, skincare, supplements, and preventive wellness is shifting from physical accessibility toward ecosystem ownership, digital engagement, personalization, trusted recommendations, and platform-based customer relationships.

# Part 2. Value Drivers

## 2.1 Revenue Growth

- **Mechanism:**  
Because PSP builds a personalized digital beauty and wellness ecosystem integrating creators, educational content, AI recommendations, and commerce, the company can increase conversion, repeat purchasing, and cross-category purchasing behavior in high-growth wellness and skincare categories.

- **Scope of impact:**  
Skincare, supplements, vitamins, wellness products, beauty-focused health products, and loyalty-driven digital purchasing.

- **Order of magnitude estimate:**  
+4% to +10% incremental category revenue within 3 years.

- **Data needed to quantify:**  
Current skincare and wellness category revenue; repeat purchasing rates; loyalty participation; digital engagement metrics; average basket size.

- **Evidence collected so far:**  
Consumers increasingly discover skincare and wellness products through influencers, social platforms, and online ecosystems; specialized beauty e-commerce and creator-driven commerce are growing rapidly among younger urban consumers.

## 2.3 Risk Reduction

- **Risk being mitigated:**  
Loss of customer attention, recommendation authority, and long-term digital engagement to specialized beauty ecosystems, influencers, and global wellness platforms.

- **Mechanism:**  
By building an integrated ecosystem, PSP strengthens direct customer relationships and reduces dependence on external discovery channels and third-party recommendation ecosystems.

- **Scope of impact:**  
Customer retention, loyalty participation, digital engagement, and long-term competitive positioning.

- **Order of magnitude estimate:**  
Potential long-term margin and engagement erosion if external platforms increasingly control beauty and wellness purchasing journeys.

- **Evidence collected so far:**  
Influencers, creator ecosystems, and specialized beauty platforms increasingly influence skincare and wellness purchasing behavior, while pharmacies risk becoming primarily fulfillment and logistics operators.

# Part 4. Key Assumptions

## Assumption 1

- **Statement:**  
Consumers will increasingly adopt digital wellness and skincare ecosystems for product discovery and purchasing behavior.

- **Basis:**  
Rapid growth of influencer-driven skincare culture, social commerce, and online wellness engagement among younger Georgian consumers.

---

## Assumption 2

- **Statement:**  
Personalized recommendations and ecosystem engagement will increase repeat purchasing and customer lifetime value.

- **Basis:**  
Platform and recommendation systems increase conversion and retention through personalization and convenience.

---

## Assumption 3

- **Statement:**  
PSP can leverage its existing loyalty systems, infrastructure, and customer trust to reduce platform adoption friction.

- **Basis:**  
PSP already possesses large customer reach, brand trust, pharmacy network infrastructure, and operational systems.

---

## Assumption 4

- **Statement:**  
Beauty, skincare, supplements, and preventive wellness categories will continue growing faster than traditional pharmaceutical retail.

- **Basis:**  
Global and regional trends show increasing prioritization of self-care, wellness lifestyles, preventive health, and personalized beauty consumption.

# Part 5. Scenario Sketch

## Base Case

- **Description:**  
PSP achieves moderate ecosystem adoption through personalized recommendations, creator partnerships, and digital wellness engagement.

- **Payback:**  
4–6 years

---

## Upside Case

- **Description:**  
Strong creator traction and ecosystem engagement significantly increase repeat purchasing, retention, and digital platform participation.

- **Payback:**  
3–4 years

---

## Downside Case

- **Description:**  
Slow customer adoption, execution complexity, and strong competition from external beauty ecosystems reduce engagement growth.

- **Payback:**  
6–8 years

---

## Key Differentiating Assumption

- **Most critical factor:**  
Recurring ecosystem engagement and adoption of personalized wellness experiences.

- **Why uncertain:**  
Georgia’s digital beauty and wellness ecosystem market is still emerging, and long-term engagement behavior is not yet fully mature.
