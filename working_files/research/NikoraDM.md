# Part 1. Strategic Rationale

### Strategic rationale (two sentences)

How should **Nikora** respond to the rapid growth of **digital commerce and on-demand grocery delivery** in order to **maintain market share and increase customer convenience through an omnichannel retail model before competitors dominate the digital channel in the next 3–5 years**?

If Nikora does not develop its own digital platform, **customers may shift to competitors that provide faster and more convenient online grocery purchasing options**, reducing Nikora’s long-term competitiveness.

### Connection to disruption diagnostic

Retail disruption is driven by **e-commerce platforms, mobile ordering, rapid delivery services, and digitally enabled customer experiences**. Online grocery services are becoming an expected convenience rather than a premium feature.

### Connection to competitive landscape

Major retailers increasingly compete not only on **price and location**, but also on **convenience, digital accessibility, and delivery speed**. If competitors introduce strong delivery platforms, Nikora risks losing customers who prioritize convenience.

# Part 2. Value Drivers

## 2.1 Revenue Growth

**Mechanism**

Because of **launching a Nikora mobile app and website for online ordering**, customers can **purchase groceries digitally and receive home delivery**, which increases **purchase frequency and attracts convenience-oriented consumers**, producing **additional revenue from digital sales channels**.

**Scope of impact**

- Urban customers (especially **Tbilisi**)
- Young professionals and busy households
- Customers who prefer **online shopping instead of visiting stores**

**Order of magnitude estimate**

Digital channels could generate **5–10% additional sales revenue within 3–4 years**.

**Data needed to quantify**

- Average Nikora store sales
- Share of grocery sales moving online
- Average online order value
- Customer adoption rate of the app

**Evidence collected so far**

Global and regional retail trends show **rapid growth of online grocery shopping and delivery services**, particularly in urban markets.

---

## 2.2 Cost Reduction

**Mechanism**

Because of **digital ordering and inventory integration**, Nikora can **improve demand forecasting and inventory management**, reducing **product waste and excess stock**, which lowers **operational costs**.

**Scope of impact**

- Inventory management
- Warehouse and store logistics
- Product waste reduction

**Order of magnitude estimate**

Potential **2–5% reduction in inventory waste and logistics inefficiencies**.

**Data needed to quantify**

- Current inventory waste rate
- Logistics costs per store
- Forecast accuracy improvements

**Evidence collected so far**

Retailers using **digital inventory systems and online ordering platforms** typically achieve better supply chain visibility and inventory planning.

---

## 2.3 Risk Reduction

**Risk being mitigated**

Loss of customers to **competitors offering online grocery shopping and delivery services**.

**Mechanism**

By introducing its own **digital ordering platform**, Nikora maintains **customer loyalty and market relevance**, reducing the risk of **market share erosion**.

**Scope of impact**

- Customer retention
- Market competitiveness
- Brand perception

**Order of magnitude estimate**

Preventing **3–5% potential revenue loss due to customer switching**.

**Data needed to quantify**

- Customer churn rates
- Competitor digital adoption rates
- Customer preference surveys

**Evidence collected so far**

Retail markets globally show that **retailers without online channels lose customers to omnichannel competitors**.

---

## 2.4 Strategic Optionality

**Future opportunity enabled**

Expansion into **full omnichannel retail**, including:

- loyalty programs
- personalized promotions
- data-driven marketing
- subscription delivery services

**Why this capability is not accessible today**

Nikora currently relies primarily on **physical retail locations**, which limits its ability to **collect customer data and offer digital engagement**.

**Mechanism**

The digital platform enables **customer data collection and analytics**, allowing Nikora to develop **personalized marketing strategies and new digital services**.

**Order of magnitude estimate**

Potential **long-term revenue increase of 10–15% through data-driven retail strategies**.

**Evidence collected so far**

Retailers that adopt **data analytics and digital customer engagement platforms** significantly improve **customer retention and average basket size**.

# Part 4. Key Assumptions

### Assumption 1 (most important)

**Statement**

We assume **10–20% of Nikora’s urban customers adopt the online ordering platform within the first three years**.

**Basis**

Adoption patterns observed in **grocery delivery services in similar urban retail markets**.

**Impact if wrong by 20%**

If adoption is lower by 20%, **revenue growth decreases and payback period extends by approximately 1–2 years**.

---

### Assumption 2

**Statement**

We assume the **average online order value is higher than in-store purchases due to basket consolidation**.

**Basis**

Online grocery shoppers typically **buy larger baskets to reduce delivery frequency**.

**Impact if wrong by 20%**

If basket size is smaller, **revenue growth decreases and ROI becomes lower**.

---

### Assumption 3

**Statement**

We assume **implementation of the platform is completed within 12–18 months**.

**Basis**

Typical timeline for **retail digital platform deployment**.

**Impact if wrong by 20%**

If implementation is delayed, **cost increases and revenue benefits start later**, reducing ROI.

# Part 5. Scenario Sketch

## 5.1 Base Case

- **Description:** Nikora launches the digital platform and achieves moderate customer adoption.
- **Key assumption value:** ~15% customer adoption
- **Rough payback period:** 3–4 years
- **Why defensible:** reflects average retail digital adoption rates.

---

## 5.2 Upside Case

- **Description:** The platform becomes widely adopted and Nikora becomes a leading omnichannel grocery retailer.
- **Key assumption value:** 25–30% adoption
- **Rough payback period:** 2–3 years
- **What must go right:** strong marketing, reliable delivery logistics, good customer experience.

---

## 5.3 Downside Case

- **Description:** Adoption is slow and implementation is delayed.
- **Key assumption value:** <10% adoption + 6-month delay
- **Rough payback period:** 5–6 years
- **Client survival:** Nikora continues relying on physical stores while improving digital services.

---

## 5.4 Key differentiating assumption

- **Key assumption:** Customer adoption rate of the online platform.
- **Why uncertain:** online grocery adoption in Georgia is still developing.
- **Research needed:** consumer surveys, competitor analysis, urban demand studies.
