# Part 1. Strategic Rationale

### Strategic rationale (two sentences)

How should Nikora Trade respond to the rapid growth of digital commerce and changing consumer shopping behavior by developing an online grocery ordering application in order to expand sales channels, improve customer convenience, and maintain competitiveness before competitors dominate the online grocery market in Georgia?

If Nikora Trade does not invest in digital retail channels, it risks losing customers to competitors offering convenient online grocery ordering and delivery services.

### Connection to disruption diagnostic

Retail disruption is driven by:

- e-commerce adoption
- mobile ordering platforms
- digital customer experience
- on-demand delivery services

Consumers increasingly prefer ordering products online rather than visiting physical stores, especially in urban markets.

### Connection to competitive landscape

Retail competitors are gradually adopting digital commerce platforms.  
Without an online ordering system, Nikora Trade risks losing market share to retailers and delivery platforms that provide online grocery services.

# Part 2. Value Drivers

## 2.1 Revenue Growth

### Mechanism

Because of the online grocery ordering application, customers will be able to order products remotely and receive delivery or store pickup.  
This increases convenience and expands the company's sales channels beyond physical store visits, generating additional revenue.

### Scope of impact

- Urban customers with mobile devices
- Busy professionals and households
- Online grocery delivery and pickup orders

### Order of magnitude estimate

3–7% revenue increase due to new online sales channels.

### Data needed to quantify

- Current total annual revenue
- Online grocery demand in Georgia
- Average order value in grocery e-commerce
- Number of potential users in urban areas

### Evidence collected so far

Global retail trends show that online grocery sales are one of the fastest-growing retail segments.

---

## 2.2 Cost Reduction

### Mechanism

Digital ordering allows better demand visibility and more efficient inventory management.  
Online ordering data improves forecasting and reduces unnecessary stock accumulation.

### Scope of impact

- Inventory planning
- Store demand forecasting
- Warehouse operations

### Order of magnitude estimate

5–10% reduction in inventory inefficiencies.

### Data needed to quantify

- Inventory holding costs
- Current demand forecasting accuracy
- Warehouse operating costs

### Evidence collected so far

Retail companies using digital sales platforms often improve inventory planning due to better demand data.

---

## 2.3 Risk Reduction

### Risk being mitigated

Loss of market share due to the growth of online retail platforms.

### Mechanism

By launching its own digital platform, Nikora Trade retains control over customer relationships and prevents dependency on third-party delivery platforms.

### Scope of impact

- Customer acquisition and retention
- Long-term competitive positioning

### Order of magnitude estimate

Preserving 2–3% of market share that could otherwise shift to competitors.

### Data needed to quantify

- Market share trends in Georgian grocery retail
- Growth of online grocery services
- Customer adoption rates of digital retail platforms

### Evidence collected so far

Many retailers globally have developed proprietary apps to maintain direct customer relationships.

---

## 2.4 Strategic Optionality

### Future opportunity enabled

Digital loyalty programs and personalized promotions.

### Why this capability is not accessible today

Nikora currently lacks a centralized digital platform that collects customer purchasing data.

### Mechanism

The mobile application collects transaction data and customer behavior data that can be used for targeted promotions and personalized offers.

### Order of magnitude estimate

Future revenue increase of approximately 2–4% through personalized marketing and loyalty programs.

### Evidence collected so far

Retailers using digital loyalty systems often experience higher customer retention and increased purchase frequency.

# Part 4. Key Assumptions

## Assumption 1 (most important)

### Statement

We assume that 10–15% of existing Nikora customers will adopt the online ordering application within the first two years.

### Basis

Observed adoption patterns of grocery mobile applications in emerging retail markets.

### Impact if wrong by 20%

If adoption reaches only 8–12%, revenue growth will be lower and the payback period will increase.

---

## Assumption 2

### Statement

We assume that the average online order value will be higher than the average in-store purchase.

### Basis

Consumers often buy multiple items when ordering groceries online.

### Impact if wrong by 20%

If online order values are lower than expected, total revenue growth decreases.

---

## Assumption 3

### Statement

We assume that the mobile platform can be fully implemented within 10–12 months.

### Basis

Typical development timelines for mid-sized retail applications.

### Impact if wrong by 20%

A delayed launch would postpone revenue generation and extend the payback period.

---

## Additional assumptions

Assumption 4: Delivery logistics can be implemented using existing store locations as distribution points.

Assumption 5: Customers will trust and adopt digital grocery shopping in the Georgian market.

# Part 5. Scenario Sketch

## Base Case

The online ordering platform achieves moderate adoption among existing Nikora customers and generates incremental revenue.

- Key assumption value used: 12% customer adoption
- Rough payback period estimate: 3 years
- Why this is the most defensible scenario: reflects typical adoption of new digital retail services.

---

## Upside Case

The mobile application becomes widely adopted and significantly increases online grocery orders.

- Key assumption value used: 20% customer adoption
- Rough payback period estimate: 1.5–2 years
- What has to go right: strong marketing, reliable delivery service, and a user-friendly application.

---

## Downside Case

Customer adoption is slow and operational challenges limit the platform’s growth.

- Key assumption value used: 6% customer adoption
- Rough payback period estimate: 5 years
- Survival strategy: continue improving the platform and gradually increase customer awareness.

---

## Key Differentiating Assumption

### The single assumption that most changes the outcome

Customer adoption rate of the online grocery application.

### Why this assumption is the most uncertain

Online grocery shopping behavior in the Georgian retail market is still developing.

### What research would reduce this uncertainty

- Market surveys of Georgian consumers
- Benchmarking adoption rates of similar retail apps
- Analysis of online food delivery usage trends.
