## Purpose

# Part 1. Porter Five Forces

## Threat of new entrants
- **Barriers to entry:**  
  High capital investment for large retail stores (approx. $2–5 million per supermarket), logistics and warehousing infrastructure (around 10–15% of operating costs), strong brand recognition of existing chains, established supplier contracts, and growing need for digital infrastructure such as e-commerce platforms and delivery systems.

- **Sources of advantage for current leaders:**  
  Existing retailers benefit from economies of scale in procurement (estimated 10–20% cost advantage), established distribution networks, strong brand loyalty, and large store networks. Major players include Carrefour, Nikora, Spar, PSP Pharmacy, Aversi Pharmacy, Alta, Elit Electronics, and local e-commerce platforms.

- **Likely new entrants:**  
  International e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Temu, AliExpress), regional online marketplaces, and local digital-first retailers. Social commerce sellers on platforms like Facebook Marketplace and Instagram also represent emerging competitors.

- **Force rating:** Medium

- **Strategic implication:**  
  While physical retail requires high investment, digital platforms reduce entry barriers. International e-commerce companies can enter the market without building physical infrastructure, increasing competitive pressure.

---

## Bargaining power of buyers
- **Who are the main buyers?**  
  Georgian households (~3.7 million population), online shoppers, and small businesses purchasing consumer goods.

- **How concentrated are they?**  
  Buyers are highly fragmented. No single buyer group dominates the market, but collectively they exert strong pressure on retailers.

- **How easy is switching?**  
  Switching costs are extremely low. Consumers can easily switch between supermarket chains, pharmacies, electronics retailers, and online marketplaces. Many consumers shop at multiple stores regularly.

- **Force rating:** High

- **Strategic implication:**  
  Retailers must compete through price promotions, product assortment, convenience, and customer experience. Loyalty programs and digital engagement are increasingly important for retaining customers.

---

## Bargaining power of suppliers
- **Who are the critical suppliers?**  
  Local agricultural producers, pharmaceutical manufacturers, global FMCG brands (Nestlé, Coca-Cola, Unilever), electronics import distributors, and logistics providers.

- **How concentrated are they?**  
  Some suppliers, particularly global brands and pharmaceutical distributors, are relatively concentrated and have strong bargaining power. Local producers are more fragmented.

- **How replaceable are they?**  
  Global brands and specialized suppliers are difficult to replace due to strong brand loyalty and product differentiation. Local suppliers are moderately replaceable.

- **Force rating:** Medium

- **Strategic implication:**  
  Retailers attempt to reduce supplier power by diversifying suppliers, sourcing locally, and developing private label products.

---

## Threat of substitutes
- **What alternatives solve the same job?**  
  Traditional open markets, neighborhood convenience stores, online marketplaces, social media sellers, and direct purchases from farmers or small producers.

- **Why would customers switch?**  
  Customers may switch due to lower prices, convenience, broader product selection, or faster delivery options.

- **Force rating:** High

- **Strategic implication:**  
  Retailers must provide competitive pricing, convenient locations, and digital shopping options to remain attractive compared to substitutes.

---

## Competitive rivalry
- **Number and type of competitors:**  
  The Georgian retail market includes grocery chains (Carrefour, Spar, Nikora, Fresco), pharmacy chains (PSP, Aversi), electronics retailers (Alta, Elit Electronics), and e-commerce platforms (Vendoo, MyMarket, Temu).

- **Basis of competition:**  
  Price discounts, product variety, convenience of store locations, customer service, and digital capabilities.

- **Price intensity:**  
  High. Retailers frequently use promotional campaigns and discounts to attract customers.

- **Innovation intensity:**  
  Moderate but increasing due to e-commerce expansion, mobile shopping applications, digital payment systems, and delivery services.

- **Force rating:** High

- **Strategic implication:**  
  Retailers must differentiate through operational efficiency, strong branding, and digital transformation to maintain competitiveness.

# Part 2. Value chain analysis

Map the chain.

| Stage | Main activities | Main actors | Profit concentration | Digital pressure points |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Production | Manufacturing and agriculture | Local producers, global manufacturers | Medium | Supply chain analytics |
| Distribution | Importing, warehousing, transport | Distributors, logistics companies | Low | Warehouse automation and tracking |
| Retail operations | Store management, merchandising | Retail chains, pharmacies, electronics stores | Medium | POS systems and data analytics |
| Sales channels | Physical stores and online platforms | Retailers and marketplaces | High | E-commerce platforms and mobile apps |

### Value chain interpretation
- **Which stage appears most vulnerable?**  
  Physical retail operations are vulnerable due to increasing competition from e-commerce platforms and digital marketplaces.

- **Which stage appears most defensible?**  
  Customer relationships and loyalty programs are more defensible because retailers can build long-term engagement and data advantages.

- **Where could a platform insert itself?**  
  Platforms can capture value in online marketplaces, delivery logistics, digital payment systems, and customer data platforms.

# Part 3. Jobs-to-be-done competition

## Core customer job
Customers want to quickly and conveniently obtain everyday products such as groceries, medicines, electronics, and household goods at affordable prices.

## Functional needs
- Wide product assortment  
- Competitive pricing  
- Convenient store locations  
- Fast checkout and payment  
- Reliable product availability  
- Online ordering and delivery options  

## Emotional or social needs
- Trust in product quality and authenticity  
- Confidence when purchasing health products from pharmacies  
- Satisfaction from finding good deals  
- Convenient and modern shopping experience  

## Alternative solutions
**Direct alternatives**
- Competing retail chains  
- Pharmacy networks  
- Electronics retailers  
- Online marketplaces  

**Indirect alternatives**
- Traditional open markets and bazaars  
- Social commerce sellers  
- International e-commerce platforms such as Amazon, Temu, and AliExpress  

## Interpretation
Competition in the Georgian retail industry extends beyond traditional retail stores. Customers can fulfill the same needs through many channels including markets, online platforms, and social commerce. As digital platforms grow, retailers increasingly compete not only with local stores but also with global e-commerce ecosystems.
