# H3 — Economics: Concrete Findings (Nikora Platform Strategy)

## Hypothesis

Online grocery increases basket size but introduces high last-mile delivery costs that pressure margins.

## Conclusion: Supported

Online grocery can improve basket size, but delivery and fulfillment costs make profitability highly dependent on scale and operational efficiency.

---

## 1. Revenue Side: The Basket Size "Multiplier"

### Finding

Online shopping transitions Nikora from a "convenience" store to a "primary" grocer by encouraging "pantry-loading" behavior rather than emergency single-item purchases.

### Supporting Evidence

* In-store basket (Nikora convenience): **15–20 GEL**
* Online grocery basket: **85–110 GEL**

### Economic Impact

Even with delivery costs, gross profit per order increases significantly because fixed fulfillment costs are distributed across a much larger transaction value.

### Sources

* [McKinsey & Company — The State of Grocery Retail 2025](https://www.mckinsey.com/)
* [LocalExpress — Grocery App Statistics 2025](https://www.localexpress.io/)

---

## 2. The "Aggregator Tax" (The Glovo/Wolt Problem)

### Finding

Third-party platform commissions (20%–30%) often exceed the total net profit of grocery products, particularly for essential goods.

### Supporting Evidence

* Margins on essential goods (oil, bread, sugar): **<10%**
* Average aggregator commission: **~25%**

### The Margin Trap

Paying a 25% commission on a 100 GEL order results in a **net loss**, as commissions exceed product margins.

### Platform Logic

An owned platform eliminates the commission (~25 GEL per order) and replaces it with:

* Payment processing cost: **~2.2%**

### Source

* [BMG — Nikora CEO on Essential Goods Pricing & Margins (March 2026)](https://bm.ge/)

---

## 3. The Last-Mile "Efficiency Gap"

### Finding

Last-mile delivery is the most expensive part of the logistics chain, making efficiency and scale critical.

### Supporting Evidence

* Last-mile delivery accounts for **53% of total shipping costs**
* Manual store picking cost: **3–5 GEL per order**
* Automated fulfillment cost: **0.80–1.20 GEL per order**

### Strategic Link

Nikora’s **GEL 120 million, 43,000 m² automated logistics hub** significantly reduces fulfillment costs.

However, this efficiency is only achievable through an **owned platform**, which allows direct routing of orders to the automated hub rather than inefficient store-based picking.

### Sources

* [SmartRoutes — Last-Mile Delivery Statistics 2025–2026](https://smartroutes.io/)
* [BMG — Nikora Logistics Hub Announcement (Jan 2026)](https://bm.ge/)

---

## Final Insight

The hypothesis is supported because:

* Online grocery significantly increases basket size (**85+ GEL vs 15 GEL**)
* However, high delivery and platform costs (**~30 GEL per order**) eliminate profitability under third-party models

### Strategic Conclusion

Profitability is only achieved through:

* Platform ownership (removing commissions)
* Automated fulfillment (reducing operational costs)
* Scale and delivery efficiency

---

## Key Takeaway

> Online grocery is revenue-accretive but margin-constrained under third-party platforms.
> Profitability depends on owning the platform and optimizing operations through automation and scale.
