# H3 — Economics: Nikora Platform Strategy

## Hypothesis

Online grocery increases basket size but introduces high last-mile delivery costs that pressure margins.

## Conclusion: Supported

Online grocery improves basket size, but delivery and fulfillment costs make profitability highly dependent on scale and operational efficiency.

---

## 1. Revenue Side: The Basket Size Multiplier

### Finding

Online shopping shifts Nikora from a convenience store model to a primary grocery provider by encouraging "pantry-loading" behavior.

### Supporting Evidence

* In-store basket: **15–20 GEL**
* Online basket: **85–110 GEL**

### Economic Impact

Larger baskets increase gross profit per order, as fixed fulfillment costs are spread over higher transaction values.

### Sources

* McKinsey & Company — *The State of Grocery Retail 2025*
* LocalExpress — *Grocery App Statistics 2025*

---

## 2. The "Aggregator Tax" (Glovo/Wolt Problem)

### Finding

Third-party platform commissions (20–30%) exceed grocery profit margins, especially for essential goods.

### Supporting Evidence

* Grocery margins (essentials): **<10%**
* Average commission: **~25%**

### Economic Impact

This creates a **negative margin structure**, where each order can result in a loss.

### Platform Logic

An owned platform eliminates commission costs, replacing them with:

* Payment processing fee: **~2.2%**

### Source

* BMG — *Nikora CEO on Essential Goods Pricing & Margins (March 2026)*

---

## 3. The Last-Mile Efficiency Gap

### Finding

Last-mile delivery is the most expensive component of logistics.

### Supporting Evidence

* Last-mile share: **53% of total shipping costs**
* Manual picking cost: **3–5 GEL/order**
* Automated fulfillment cost: **0.80–1.20 GEL/order**

### Strategic Insight

Nikora’s **GEL 120M automated logistics hub** significantly reduces fulfillment costs.

### Key Dependency

This efficiency is only achievable with an **owned platform**, which enables direct routing to the automated warehouse.

### Sources

* SmartRoutes — *Last-Mile Delivery Statistics 2025–2026*
* BMG — *Nikora Logistics Hub Announcement (Jan 2026)*

---

## Final Insight

The hypothesis is supported because:

* Online grocery significantly increases basket size (**~85+ GEL vs 15 GEL**)
* However, high delivery and platform costs (**~30 GEL per order**) eliminate profitability

### Strategic Conclusion

Profitability is only achievable through:

* Platform ownership (eliminating commissions)
* Automated fulfillment (reducing costs)
* Operational scale and efficiency

---

## Key Takeaway

> Online grocery is revenue-accretive but margin-destructive under third-party platforms.
> Profitability requires a fully integrated, owned platform model.
