# Part 1. Process Redesign

## Selected Process: Skincare Recommendation-to-Purchase Journey

This is the process most transformed by the ecosystem strategy. Currently it is fragmented across offline store staff, social media discovery, and unconnected loyalty data. The ecosystem strategy fuses recommendation, personalization, and fulfillment into a single AI-mediated journey.

---

## Current State: The Process as It Operates Today

**Process name:** Customer skincare product recommendation and purchase

| Decision point | Who decides | Information used | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Product discovery | Customer (self-directed) or in-store pharmacist | Personal knowledge, shelf placement, marketing materials | Customer picks wrong product category; no personalization; high return/dissatisfaction rate |
| 2. Recommendation relevance | In-store skincare advisor or pharmacist | Visual skin assessment, verbal customer input, brand training | Advice is inconsistent across staff; no record of prior purchases or skin history |
| 3. Cross-sell / upsell | Cashier or store manager | Real-time basket contents only | Missed revenue; no linkage to loyalty data or AI-driven affinity scoring |
| 4. Loyalty reward application | CRM/Loyalty system (automated) + cashier | Loyalty card scan, purchase total | Loyalty points applied but not used to drive personalized re-engagement; no behavioral segmentation |

### Current process performance

- Average time from discovery to purchase decision: 15–25 minutes in-store; no structured digital journey exists
- Error rate / rework rate: High mismatch between purchased products and skin needs (estimated from industry benchmarks at 25–35% of first-time skincare buyers)
- Customer impact of current failure modes: Low repeat purchase rate in skincare/wellness category; customer exits ecosystem after single transaction; no data capture for future personalization

---

## Target State: The Redesigned Process

| Decision point | AI handles | Human handles | Escalation threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Product discovery | AI surfaces personalized product sets based on skin profile, purchase history, and influencer/dermatologist content engagement | Influencer or dermatologist creates discovery content that feeds the AI training layer | Customer has zero prior data (first-time user with no profile): human skincare advisor completes onboarding intake via app chat |
| 2. Recommendation relevance | AI recommendation engine scores product-skin profile match using behavioral data, purchase history, and advisor input | Dermatologist partner reviews and approves AI recommendation logic for medical-grade skincare sub-categories | Customer query involves a prescription-linked product, a reported allergic reaction, or a condition flagged as requiring medical review |
| 3. Cross-sell / upsell | AI generates basket expansion suggestions at checkout based on affinity model and category uplift logic | None required for standard cross-sell | Customer basket value exceeds GEL 200 and includes two or more new product categories simultaneously |
| 4. Loyalty reward application | AI triggers personalized re-engagement campaigns based on purchase recency, frequency, and category affinity score | CRM team reviews campaign performance weekly and adjusts segmentation thresholds | Customer has not purchased in 60 days and was previously a top-quartile spender |

### Target process performance

- Expected time from discovery to purchase decision: 3–7 minutes via app
- Expected error rate: Product-skin mismatch reduced to 8–12%
- Recommendation systems improve repeat purchase and basket size through personalization logic
- Personalized engagement strengthens long-term customer retention and ecosystem participation

## Single Most Important Structural Change

### Change
PSP should appoint a dedicated Chief Digital Officer (CDO) or Head of Ecosystem with cross-functional authority over Digital, CRM, Marketing, Partnerships, and AI deployment initiatives.

### Reason
AI personalization requires real-time data sharing and governance coordination across multiple functions. Without centralized ecosystem ownership, recommendation quality, implementation speed, and customer experience consistency may deteriorate due to siloed decision-making.

### Horizon
H1 — must be implemented before ecosystem scaling.

### Estimated Cost
GEL 80,000–120,000 annually.

# Updated Operating Model Logic

The operating model supports the phased transformation roadmap and ensures that AI-enabled personalization capabilities are introduced only after PSP establishes the required organizational, technical, and customer-data foundations.

Horizon 1 focuses on building the operational backbone of the ecosystem through customer data governance, MVP deployment, partnership activation, content production, loyalty incentives, and organizational change management.

Horizon 2 expands the ecosystem into AI-driven personalization and monetization through recommendation engines, advisor services, segmentation, sponsored campaigns, and ecosystem performance tracking.

Horizon 3 represents long-term strategic option space rather than immediate commitment. Advanced AI skin analysis, premium wellness services, and broader preventive healthcare expansion should only be activated after measurable ecosystem adoption and engagement.

---

# Governance Logic

The ecosystem requires significantly higher cross-functional coordination than PSP’s traditional retail operating structure. AI personalization depends on continuous interaction between Digital Product, CRM, Marketing, Partnerships, Medical Advisors, and Data teams.

The Chief Digital Officer (CDO) or Head of Ecosystem is responsible for:

- ecosystem governance
- cross-functional coordination
- AI deployment prioritization
- customer data standards
- roadmap execution oversight
- KPI accountability
- escalation management

Without centralized governance, personalization initiatives risk fragmentation across business units, reducing recommendation quality and slowing ecosystem implementation.

---

# Performance Logic

The operating model shifts PSP performance measurement toward ecosystem engagement and long-term customer value creation.

Key ecosystem KPIs include:

- repeat purchase rate
- recommendation acceptance rate
- customer lifetime value
- skincare and wellness engagement
- loyalty participation
- creator-to-purchase conversion
- ecosystem retention rate
- advisor engagement activity
- personalized campaign performance

The ecosystem dashboard consolidates these KPIs into a centralized performance-monitoring layer for executive decision-making.

---

# Strategic Logic

The ecosystem is not designed as a standalone business separate from PSP’s current operations. Instead, it functions as a digital enhancement layer built on top of PSP’s existing pharmacy infrastructure, loyalty ecosystem, logistics capabilities, healthcare credibility, and customer base.

The operating model therefore prioritizes:

- phased implementation
- MVP-first deployment
- moderate initial investment
- API-based AI integration
- ecosystem scalability
- low operational complexity during early stages

The phased structure reduces financial and execution risk while allowing PSP to validate ecosystem adoption before expanding into more advanced AI-enabled healthcare and wellness services.
